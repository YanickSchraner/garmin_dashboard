# Product Guidelines: Garmin Custom Dashboard

## Design & Aesthetic
- **Health & Wellness Focus**: Use warm, calming colors and layouts that prioritize well-being, recovery, and consistent habits over raw "performance" metrics.
- **System-Aware Theme**: Support both Light and Dark modes, automatically adapting to the user's OS settings for a seamless experience.

## UX & Interaction
- **Single-Page Glanceable Dashboard**: The primary view must provide all essential metrics (104-run goal, weekly summary) in a single, high-level layout suitable for quick status checks.
- **Multi-view Detailed Exploration**: Provide secondary, deeper views for analyzing health trends over the year (e.g., sleep duration vs. RHR trends).
- **Responsive Layout**: Ensure the dashboard scales effectively from mobile devices to desktop monitors.

## Tone & Voice
- **Friendly & Motivational**: Use an engaging tone that celebrates progress. Example: "You've completed 2 runs this week—keep it up!" instead of "Completed: 2".
- **Clear Metric Labels**: While friendly, avoid jargon and ensure that data labels (e.g., "RHR", "Target vs. Actual") are clear and understandable for all users.
